# <center> Chapter 1 </center>

It was the first day of class in 11th grade, the air is hung thick with anticipation, a tangible blend of youthful
uncertainty and hope for the year ahead. The teacher's voice, echoed around the classroom as he instructed everyone
to introduce themselves, and draw a map of their house from school. Their names, followed by dreams whispered to
the eager faces that shimmered in the sunbeams through the window.

Damien took a seat at the front. Quiet, observing, and felt out of sync with his classmates. He held a crumpled sheet
of paper, and drew a not so typical childish doodle. He drew a meticulous three-dimensional model, of all the roads,
the trees, and the houses around his hometown, with each line and curve meticulously rendered. Damien traced the
outline of his house, and simply left it there. He didn't label it.

Dahlia, stood over him, and gaze curoius as she observed his work. *"Nice map!"*, she said with a touch of amusement.
Leaning in to get a better look at the intricate details, asks *"Where's your house by the way?"*

Damien pointed his finger towards the sprawling massive trees, with a slight smirk in his lips. *"Somewhere, right
here."*

The words hung in the air, heavy and ambiguous. A silence fell upon the room as Damien's classmates stared at him
as the teacher points at him to be the next one to introduce himself. Their eyes wide with a mixture of confusion and
intrigue.

He rose to introduce himself. *"My name is Damien"*, he announced with a voice, hesitant for a moment. *"I live near
this school, right there..."*. He paused, as he's about to say his dream. *"And my dream is..., I'm kinda working
on it."*. His words seemed to tumble out of him, the weight of unspoken possibilities wheighing heaviliy on his
shoulders.

Dahlia's laughter pierced the awkward silence, as a sharp sound that echoed through the room.

Damien sank back into his seat, a silent sigh escaping his lips. The world felt strangely, not the
usual jittery first day buzz, but something deeper, more unsettling. His eyes met Dahlia's gaze, the unspoken
question hanging heavy between, what was his dream and where did it lead?

*"Any questions?"* the teacher asked, his grin widening as he leaned against the heavy wooden desk. The room, which
had been stiff with the formality of introductions, began to loosen.

A hand went up in the middle row. *"Sir, what subject do you actually teach?"*

*"I’m the math teacher,"* he replied, relishing the collective groan that followed. He held up a hand before the
complaints could start. *"But don't worry—I'm not your class advisor. My job is to make you solve for X, not to manage
your lives."*

A ripple of relieved laughter went through the room. *"Then give us a math fact, Sir!"* another student shouted.
*"Something to make it less boring."*

The teacher tapped a finger against his temple. *"A math fact... well, did you know the first mathematician was actually
mentioned in the Bible?"*

The class grew quiet, skeptical but curious. 

*"It was Moses"* the teacher said, his face a mask of mock-seriousness. *"He came up with the very first equation
: c/2."*

It took a second for the pun to sink in—the Red Sea, divided by two—and then the room dissolved into laughter. Even
the students in the back who had been trying to look cool cracked a smile. 

Damien, however, remained a statue in the corner. He didn't laugh. He didn't even blink. He just stared at the intricate,
unlabeled map on his desk, his thumb tracing the jagged line of a road that led nowhere.

*"Alright, alright,"* the teacher said, waving them off. *"Since it's the first day and the administration is still
figuring out where to put you all, take a break. Recess starts now."*

The classroom emptied in a flurry of movement and loud chatter. But Damien didn't join them. He stayed in his seat,
the silence of the room wrapping around him like a familiar shroud. He preferred the empty desks to the crowded hallways.

He was sketching a small, precise tree on the margin of his paper when the door creaked. Dahlia walked back in, a
small milk carton in her hand. She paused, her gaze drifting toward him before she approached with a hesitant, warm smile.

*"Mind if I sit here for a bit?"* she asked softly, pointing to the empty chair beside him.

Damien looked up, his expression a neutral mask. *"If you want."*

She sat down, her presence a quiet hum next to his stillness. She didn't try to force a conversation, for which he
was grateful. But after a few minutes, he saw her leaning forward, her brow furrowed as she squinted at the chalkboard.
She tilted her head, trying to see past the harsh glare of the morning sun reflecting off the green surface. 

Without a word, she stood up, giving him a small, apologetic shrug. *"I think I’ll head back to my old seat. Easier
to see the board from there."*

Damien watched her go, his eyes returning to his desk. He knew why she’d left. He’d chosen this seat specifically
because it was tucked into a blind spot—the glare of the window made half the board unreadable, and the angle was
cramped. It was a seat designed for someone who didn't want to be seen, and didn't care to see anything else.

He stayed there, a lone island in the front row, long after the others returned and the teacher announced they were
being dismissed early. 

He walked home alone, the sun-drenched streets feeling like a foreign landscape. To the groups of laughing students
around him, he was just another face in the crowd, a stranger in a world that moved too fast for him to care.
